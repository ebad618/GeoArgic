Corn Oil

About Corn Oil

Corn oil is a popular vegetable oil extracted from the germ of corn kernels. It is commonly used in cooking for its mild flavor and high smoke point, making it ideal for frying, saut√©ing, baking, and salad dressings.

Health Benefits

  • Rich in polyunsaturated fats
  • Contains Vitamin E
  • Supports heart health
  • Helps reduce cholesterol levels

Nutritional Information (per 1 tbsp)

  • Calories: 120
  • Total Fat: 14g
  • Saturated Fat: 1.5g
  • Polyunsaturated Fat: 8g
  • Monounsaturated Fat: 3g

How Corn Oil is Made

The oil is extracted from corn germ through mechanical pressing and/or solvent extraction. It then undergoes refining processes to remove impurities, odor, and color, producing a clear, light oil perfect for cooking.

Corn Oil vs. Other Oils

  • Olive Oil: Corn oil has a higher smoke point.
  • Canola Oil: Corn oil contains more polyunsaturated fats.
  • Coconut Oil: Corn oil is lower in saturated fat.

Fun Facts

  • Used in the production of biodiesel.
  • Often found in margarine and processed foods.
  • One of the most affordable oils available.
